My Learning Plan

*What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

my long term goal is to one day own a business that brings together the different sectors of tech into one place but is still accesible to the public

*A description of your own strengths and limitations in relation to learning:

my limitations in learning is my attention span and need to have a more practicle oriented learning, as i learn and understand different concepts better when i can actively apply them rather then just pure theory.

my strengths are being adaptable to whatever situatuion i'm placed in and also my ability to multi-task as it allows me to both understand the theory while applying it in a practicle sense.

*What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

i would like to learn more leadership and team oriented skills as that would not only allow to grow what knowledge i already have but would also allow me to apply them to a new field and group of people.

*A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme. This should include:

i)An explanation of how you will manage yourself to work productively and safely with other learners, facilitators and industry/community representatives:
i would set timeframes and working hours for myself, as well as break times. setting up these plans would allow to continue to have a workflow that matches that of real work places, while also allowing me to grow my interpersonal skills.

ii)What you will do when things are building up and starting to get to you:

i would take short breaks and do something else that might be easier or simply removing myself and coming back to it later with a calmer mind, which typically helps me to problem solve more effectively.

*A commitment as to how and where you will seek help in a timely way

i'm not one who is shy or afraid to ask for help but i do prefer to run through other options first, but if i do feel myself becoming unable to work or move forward then i would easily ask for assistance.

*A description of what you expect from the facilitation team:

just an open communication line and active times to speak to students who need help.

*Any scheduling information such as block-out times when you are committed to other things. For example, you might have whānau commitments like school drop-offs. It’s important to plan these in.

for myself its generally after 5pm and sundays, as i do dinners at night plus i have church on sunday and save that day for family time.
